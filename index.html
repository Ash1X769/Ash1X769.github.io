    🧠 Why Spaced Repetition Works

    ScienceSpark uses spaced repetition — a scientifically proven technique that schedules review at the optimal moment before forgetting occurs. Cards answered correctly are shown less often; harder cards more frequently. Research shows this improves long-term retention by up to 200% compared to re-reading notes.

    🔥 The Power of Streaks

    Daily consistency beats marathon sessions. Even 10 minutes a day, every day, is more effective than 2 hours once a week. Encourage your child to maintain their streak — the habit builds the skill. Celebrate consecutive days, not just big scores.

    💬 How to Support at Home

    Ask them to explain a topic to you — the "teach it back" method is one of the most powerful revision strategies. You don't need to know the science; just ask "can you tell me about..." and listen. If they can explain it clearly, they know it.

    📱 Best Time to Study

    Studies suggest early evening (after dinner but before 9pm) is optimal for memory consolidation before sleep. A short 10-minute session at the same time each day builds a sustainable habit. Avoid screens for 30 minutes before bed after studying.

    ⚠️ The Highlighting Trap

    Passive revision — re-reading notes, highlighting, watching videos — feels productive but produces poor exam results. Active recall (being tested, like in ScienceSpark) is far more effective. Encourage your child to close their notes and test themselves.
